                        Follow the path down the cave until you exit at the bottom. DON'T run straight out. There is a Yharnamite at the end of the path with three turret guns that will fire in sequence straight down the middle of the path. To avoid the shots, wait until the middle gun has fired, then duck between cover on the LEFT side of the path. No fire is directed down the left of the path. Be careful though, halfway down the path, next to the Amygdala skull there is a pressure pad which will fire a hidden turret at you. When you can get close enough, attack the turrets to take them out of action, (there is a 5th turret in the area aiming directly at the second Yharnhamite) then kill the two Yharnhamites. Continue down the path to find three more Yharnamites and another Boom Hammer hunter. The Yharnamites will throw Delayed Molotovs at you, which have a several second fuse before they blow. Keep an eye on where they throw them in relation to you. You're going to want to pull the Boom Hammer hunter up to the area where the turrets are to have enough room to fight him effectively. Once they're all dealt with, there's a small section lower than the main path with an item and another Yharnamite.

                                            I don't know how to edit this page but here's a strategy. Items To Bring: Bolt Paper Suggested Weapon: Ludwig's Holy Blade (Obtained by finding a 'blade of light' badge located at the end of the optional path up the elevator in the safe area of the Cathedral. There are many gunners and black-clothed men hiding in darker spaces.) Benefits of Ludwig's Holy Blade: The weapon transformation is essentially a ultra greatsword- but the R2 charge attack packs immense damage and the standard mode is aggressive and fast. Fighting The Boss: Upon your first time engaging Rom his health bar and name won't appear- and he'll be passive. After you die and re-enter the dungeon he'll be auto aggressive. Phase One: Rom is rather helpless in round one, summoning a group of spiders who can easily take a third of your health out or one shot you if they flank your back. Never turn your back to the spiders. The spiders can jump at you and land on their heads, exposing their backsides. Hitting them when they're standing on their heads is an instant kill. Flanking spiders is a one to two hit kill. Taking a spider from the front is somewhat safe, but their heads act as a physical guard. A three hit combo will allow two hits to chip their 'masks' and the third strike will deal the final blow. A moveset to note: If a spider jumps to the side, it will follow up with a jump forward. If spiders raise their legs, back up and avoid their continuous combo. When most of the spiders are dispatch they'll slowly mimic the bodily movements of Rom, allowing you to flank them easily as they panic in their slow movement. When the spiders are dead, pop a bolt paper and switch to greatsword mode. Charge the R2 attack when you have a good shot at his side, and yes you'll have to lead the shot some. Proceed to keep wailing on the boss until he begins to glow and spin, when he does this you can snap in one to two shots before he completely disappears. Pan your camera until you see him drop in with his spiders again- but this time you need to run to him asap. Phase Two: Rom will have three new tricks up his sleeve- a pair of arcane magic attacks and an area of effect detonation. The first attack is where he'll hail crystal/ice chunks at you from a diagonal, aerial angle. This is indicated when he rears up on his hind legs. The best way to avoid this is to either spam your roll dodge or run directly toward him. The second attack is indicated by an eccentric rolling around motion, where the same hunks of ice/crystal will rise from the ground wherever the ground turns blue. This can be easily avoided if you patrol the outside 'border' of the spider formation. You must dispatch the spiders as you have before while calmly pacing your walking around the ground shards. The aerial volley occurs mostly when you're too far away- but it happens at random in close range. The ground volley rises frequently when dispatching the spiders, but stay calm. Three hit the spiders safely while keeping an eye on the boss- don't take the risk of flanking spiders and wait for them to headbutt for free kills. When the spiders are dispatched and you close in, be wary of his area of effect explosion (similar to Praal but smaller). Use a bolt paper and lead a charged R2 into his side. Spam him with light attack with either mode of the weapon you see fit until he disappears again. Phase 3: The same rules apply- only Rom will now ttempt to charge you or flail around in an attempt to stunlock you for the spiders to follow up on. Rinse and repeat, the bolt paper is the saving grace. Item Drops: The spiders will drop madman knowledge on occasion. Strategy provided by TorbyssTeck (PSN: TKLusticar)
