            Kos Parasite is a trick weapon in Bloodborne. It was added with The Old Hunters DLC. There are 3 versions of this weapon: Normal, Uncanny and Lost. The only known differences being the Gem Imprints and the locations in which they are found.

             

            Kos Parasite Description

            "When the carcass of Kos washed up on the coast, its insides were teeming with tiny parasites, unlike any found in humans.
            This atypical weapon can only be clasped tight and swung, but a Kos Parasite is said to stimulate phantasms inhabiting a lumenwood"

             

            Kos Parasite Information

            • Transforms from right-hand tentacle to dual-wield tentacles.
            • Requires 16 Blood Stone Shards, 16 Twin Blood Shards, 16 Chunks and 1 Blood Rock 3,720 Blood Echoes to fully upgrade.
            • You must equip the Milkweed Rune to use this weapon effectively, granting a wider moveset, greatly extended range and an appreciable damage multiplier, far more than is displayed on the weapon. This will transform the player fully into a Lumenwood.
            • To perform a Leap Attack, tap forward on the Left Stick as you press R2
            • To perform a Transformation Attack, press L1 while performing another attack.
            • Whilst the weapon and the Rune are equipped, the player's dash and rolling animations change to become faster and lighter.
            • Without the Rune, the character will have the regular unarmed moveset when using this weapon, however the Arcane damage will be added, meaning it can still be effectively used with good enough gems.
            • For unarmed moveset, only right hand has enhanced unarmed damage,

             

            Location

            Normal

            • Dropped by Orphan of Kos

            Uncanny Kos Parasite

            • Purchased at Bath Messenger in any Depth 5 Non-Root Chalice Dungeon (level 3) for 60000 Blood Echoes. (Also confirmed on NG+)
            • If you have the normal version: Create Glyph 8i29rqz3 (Pthumeru Labyrinth) and buy from Bath Messengers up a ladder from the main room for 60,000 Blood Echoes.

            Lost Kos Parasite

            • Purchased at Bath Messenger in any Depth 5 Non-Root Chalice Dungeon (level 3) for 60000 Blood Echoes. (Also confirmed on NG+)
            • If you have the normal version: Create Glyph 8i29rqz3 (Pthumeru Labyrinth) and buy from Bath Messengers up a ladder from the main room for 60,000 Blood Echoes.

             

            Player Notes

            • Its trickmode L2 casts a multi-hitting arcane attack for 2 quicksilver bullets, very similar to a smaller Call from Beyond.
            • Backdash R1 in both modes spits a short range projectile forwards from the mouth that causes slow poison build-up.
            • Jumping attack in transformed mode attacks with your tongue/tentacle.
            • Transform attack back to normal mode fires a short range mass of parasites forward.
            • This weapon will not cause you to recoil backwards after you hit a wall. Coupling this with it's great reach, the Kos Parasite is great for attacking through walls or around obstacles.
            • This weapon has a high amount of stagger with every hit, equal to the Beast Cutter. Since the weapon is slow, it makes up for it with making every human-sized enemy or those slightly larger prone to flinching with every hit (regardless of the type of attack). It's ideal as a bait-and-punish tactic weapon and/or "hit & run".
            • Be wary of this weapon's weaknesses, of which there are many. Neutral light attacks are altogether cumbersome to get a handle over, being slow with a mediocre, narrow range and large, easily punished recovery periods between them.

            Trivia

            • The Kos Parasite is perhaps the most interestingly unique weapon in Bloodborne in various ways: It is one of two weapons that can reach it's full potential while using a rune (the other being Beast Claw), the weapon itself is not held, it is purely an arcane weapon, it is the only weapon directly dropped by defeating a boss, it has the highest durability of any weapon at 600 respectively, it is the only weapon to have an A-scaling of any weapon without any upgrades and is the only weapon to have an S-scaling by upgrading it twice.
            • It shares other features with the Beast Claw in addition to the Rune synergy, both are tied for the cheapest weapons to fully upgrade at 3,720 Blood Echoes required.

                                                                          Very underrated weapon. It's basically a heavy version of the whip, slower but with similar range plus poise on its attacks and high stagger. It's a better choice than the Beast Cutter, which is too slow in my opinion (in whip form anyway). The dashing/rolling R1 attack has super long reach and poise, which makes it the best dashing R1 attack in the game. The L2 is insane, dealing very high damage in a large area around you, and it also has poise. You can clear out groups of enemies very quickly with it, and it only costs 2 bullets. Most enemies are staggered by its regular transformed R1s. I would say its only real weakness is lower DPS compared to some other weapons, but if you are stunlocking things or hitting them out of range it doesn't matter. For large enemies who aren't easily staggered, I recommend using the slightly faster 1 handed R1s. Overall it's super satisfying to slap enemies around with it. One of my favorite weapons.
