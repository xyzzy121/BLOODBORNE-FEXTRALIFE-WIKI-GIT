            Simon's Bowblade is a Trick Weapon in Bloodborne. It was introduced in The Old Hunters Expansion. There are 3 versions of this weapon: Normal, Uncanny and Lost. The only known differences being the Gem Imprints and the locations in which they are found

             

            Simon's Bowblade Description

            "Choice weapon of Simon, one of the first Healing Church hunters.

            Simon despised firearms, and so the Church workshop had this specially fashioned to his liking. The large curved blade serves as a bow when transformed.

            But aside from a few close friends, Simon was scoffed at for his choice of arms, for who would dare face the beasts with a measly bow?"

             

             

            Simon's Bowblade Information

            • Transforms from a one-handed blade to a bow
            • Firing the bow will consume a single quicksilver bullet.
            • The Normal variant is best for players who wish to use both forms of the weapon.
            • The Uncanny variant can function as an alternative to the Normal, however elemental gems are wasted on this weapon.
            • The Lost variant is ideal for those who want to maximize the bow's blood attack, due to its circular gem slot, typically only found on guns.

             

            Location

            Normal

            • Fishing Hamlet: Found on the body of Simon the Harrowed next to the Lighthouse Hut lantern after answering the first option at the Hunter's Nightmare shortcut and going through his quest-line. Interact with him as he lies on the ground to obtain the weapon.
            • You may also kill Simon the Harrowed at The Hunter's Nightmare or Research Hall to obtain the weapon.

            Uncanny Simon's Bowblade

            • Glyph 8i29rqz3 (Pthumeru Chalice) Bath Messengers. Must have Normal version to purchase for 24 000 blood echoes.
            • Isz Chalice: Bath Messengers. Must have the Normal version to purchase for 24 000 blood echoes. Bath is located in layer 3, up a ladder just before the lever to the boss.

            Lost Simon's Bowblade

            • Glyph 8i29rqz3 (Pthumeru Chalice) Bath Messengers. Must have Normal version to purchase for 24 000 blood echoes.
            • Isz Chalice: Bath Messengers. Must have the Normal version to purchase for 24 000 blood echoes. Bath is located in layer 3, up a ladder just before the lever to the boss.

             

             

            Player Notes

            • The Bow's charge attack into an enemy's back can stagger them, setting up an easy visceral attack by your cooperator.
            • Your blade's damage is based on the physical damage of the weapon.
            • Your bow's damage is based on the bloodtinge damage of the weapon.
            • You should most definitely use Phys. ATK UP gems as they affect both physical and bloodtinge damage.
            • You can use the Monocular to accurately Aim the Bow as you would in Dark Souls. This allows for Free Form sniping.
            • Since it's introduction, the Bladebow has become an almost infamous weapon amongst avid PvP players due to it's perceived ease of use and scant weaknesses. The weapon sports a dual "S" Scaling in both Skill and Bloodtinge, fair reach, a wide, circuitous swing, coupled with a fair period of recovery. On top of being able to hold it's own up close, ranged attacks are quick enough to demand constant attention on the part of an opponent as well as discourage deserters, making it capable of adapting to most ranges.

            Trivia

            • Simon's Bowblade is the only weapon to have a S-scaling for two attributes (Skill and Bloodtinge respectively) when fully upgraded

                                        "who would dare face the beast with a measly bow?"

                                        Well, this bow is far from ordinary. The complex mechanism of the bow propels the particularly big and heavy arrow at the same speed as the pistol's bullets and when pulling the string with strong attacks, the bow bends a little to allow extra power on release.

                                        Not only that, but take a look at the arrow's design. It's telescopic handle fully extends with the strong attack, and remains partially extended mid-flight. This means it gains extra power on hit when the handle fully retracts, allowing the arrow to dig in the flesh further using the extra kinetic force from the flight.

                                        So yeah, whoever weilds a bow capable of making ebrietas cry would have no trouble with beasts.

These gemstones allow your character to heal continuously without the use of blood vials or the Choir bell. Thirdly, farm enough to get a lost BowBlade. Unless you're lucky enough to get a radial as well, but the lost is a better, more assured bet. Fourth, enjoy your infinite bullets in exchange for your time. Though you won't do too much damage at one time, you have infinite bullets. This also allows you to continuously use some Hunter Tools for continuous damage, and extend usage of the more expensive ones. For example, with blood bullets you can effectively infinitely use the Hunter's Bone. Might as well have fully learned it, because now you can infinitely cast it! Augur of Ebrietas, while not the most damage, can also parry and backstab at range, and this only uses one bullet per use. Executioners glove does about as much as 3 charged R2 shots, yet they're slower, but they have more tracking and cost about the same. A Call Beyond and Tiny Tonitrus cost about 1, 2 quick silver bullets respectively. Combining all of this with Oedon Writhe and Blood Rapture, you have absolute infinite casting.
